"In the name of Jesus,
get in the car."

A UK pastor was arrested for street preaching. An officer mocked him with those words. Scripture says something else about who is really in authority.

Acts 5:29 · Isaiah 33:22

Scroll ↓

Steve Maile is 66 years old. He is a grandfather. He pastors Oasis City Church in Watford, England. On April 18, while preaching the gospel in the town centre, he was handcuffed so tightly he needed medical splints afterward, bundled into a police car while his family watched, and mocked by an officer who told him, "In the name of Jesus, get in the car."

The charge was a Section 5 public order offence. The real offence was saying that Jesus is Lord in a public square that belongs to Him.

This is not a human rights story. It is a sovereignty story.

The state operates as if authority rises from the ballot box or the police station. The Bible says something older and more terrifying.

For the LORD is our Judge, the LORD is our Lawgiver, the LORD is our King; He will save us.

Isaiah 33:22

God does not share His throne. Human governments are His ministers, not His rivals. They possess delegated authority, strictly limited by His decree. When a magistrate forbids what God commands, the magistrate has not issued a law. He has issued a blasphemy.

Peter and the apostles faced the same thing. The Sanhedrin ordered them to stop preaching. Their response was not a petition for religious liberty. It was a declaration of war on the wrong side.

We ought to obey God rather than men.

Acts 5:29

To stop preaching because a human official says so is nothing short of mutiny against Christ's headship.

But here is where Reformed theology refuses the easy narrative. We do not say that God was surprised by this arrest. We say what the early church prayed after Herod and Pontius Pilate conspired to murder Jesus:

For truly against Your holy Servant Jesus, whom You anointed, both Herod and Pontius Pilate, with the Gentiles and the people of Israel, were gathered together to do whatever Your hand and Your purpose determined before to be done.

Acts 4:27-28

The same hand that holds the galaxies holds the Hertfordshire police badge. This is not fatalism. It is providence.

So we do not panic. 2 Timothy 2:9 says the word of God is not chained.

John Huss taught that "to resist him is not a sin, it is a mandate." Martin Luther said: "Here I stand." John Calvin wrote that Christ alone is the Head over all things to the church.

They were not fighting for religious freedom. They were fighting for the freedom of God to be God.

The officer who mocked Steve Maile treated the name of the risen King like a parking ticket.

He will learn better. Every knee will bow. The question is not whether Jesus is King. The question is whether the Hertfordshire Police will recognize it now, or on the day when recognition comes too late.

The state may have the keys to a cell. Christ has the keys to death and Hades. Preach anyway.
