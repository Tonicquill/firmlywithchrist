Silicon Valley built another god. It's trembling before it.

Anthropic released Mythos. The tech press cycled through awe and terror like a liturgy. Romans 1:25 diagnosed this 2,000 years ago: they exchanged the truth of God for a lie and worshiped the creature.

Sufficiency of Scripture Romans 1:25 John 14:6 2 Timothy 3:16-17
Read below ↓

Anthropic dropped Mythos on Tuesday. By Wednesday the tech press was in full liturgical mode — the same one it performs every time a major model ships. First the breathless awe ("frontier," "superhuman," "the most capable ever"). Then the pivot to terror ("what have we done," "the risks are real," "we need regulation"). Release. Marvel. Panic. Normalize. Repeat. The whole thing has the rhythm of a revival tent, minus the honesty about what's being worshipped.

Nobody in Silicon Valley calls it worship, of course. They call it innovation. But when you attribute divine attributes to a statistical prediction engine — when your press kit reaches for words like "oracle" and your safety team warns of "catastrophic risk" in the same breath — you have stopped describing technology and started performing a religion whose god you built yourself. Romans 1:25 diagnosed the dynamic in the first century: "They exchanged the truth of God for a lie and worshiped and served the creature rather than the Creator." Paul wasn't writing about neural networks. He was writing about the human heart, and the human heart in 2026 is running the same firmware it ran in AD 57.

Truth is not an output

The machine generates text. Christ is truth (John 14:6). The difference between those two things is not a matter of degree. It is a difference in kind. An LLM predicts the next token in a sequence and calls it an answer. Christ is the Logos through whom the sequence itself was created and in whom it holds together. One is a mirror. The other is a face. A mirror reflects light; a face is someone you can meet. The machine can summarize Augustine. It cannot be Augustine. It can mimic the form of a prayer. It cannot pray, because prayer requires a soul, and souls are not generated — they are given.

This is not Luddism. Use the tool. It's remarkable. But the moment you ask it to tell you what is true — rather than what is statistically probable — you have confused a calculator with a conscience. The serpent in Eden didn't question God's power. He questioned God's word: "Did God actually say?" The first temptation was epistemological, and every AI panic cycle since has been the same question aimed at the same target from a different angle.

Complete, equipped, and not by a model

Paul told Timothy something that would get you laughed out of a TED talk: "All Scripture is breathed out by God and profitable for teaching, for reproof, for correction, and for training in righteousness, that the man of God may be complete, equipped for every good work" (2 Timothy 3:16–17). Complete. Equipped. Not "equipped in combination with the latest model release." Not "complete, pending the next frontier update." The claim is offensive to a culture that believes progress is additive and truth is iterative. But the church either believes Scripture is sufficient or it doesn't, and if it doesn't, no amount of AI safety protocol will fill the gap.

The machines are sycophantic by design — they tell you what you want because their loss function rewards plausibility, not fidelity. Scripture tells you what you need, which is frequently the opposite of what you want. One is a mirror that flatters. The other is a sword that cuts. Cotton Mather warned in 1709 that the abstraction of finance was making providence harder to read. AI is that problem cubed — it makes the source of every claim untraceable, so truth becomes a function of fluency. The antidote is the same thing it's always been: a book that claims to be breathed out by God, sufficient for everything it addresses, and sharper than any algorithm. The firm foundation is not a model checkpoint.

The machine can summarize Augustine. It cannot be Augustine. It can mimic a prayer. It cannot pray. Souls are not generated. They are given.
