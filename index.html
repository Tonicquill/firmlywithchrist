They submitted every document. The permit never came.

160 Christians in East Kalimantan did everything the government asked. The permit never came. The Reformers would recognize this.

Ecclesiology Acts 4:19-20 Acts 5:29 Matthew 16:18 Hebrews 10:25
Read below ↓

The Toraja Church in Samarinda Seberang, East Kalimantan, has 160 members. They applied for a building permit in 2023. They obtained a recommendation from the Interfaith Harmony Forum in September 2024. They obtained approval from the Ministry of Religious Affairs in April 2025. Every document the Indonesian government asked for, they submitted. The permit never came.

The local government says some community groups oppose the church. The congregation says that is discrimination. They plan to report the case to the Ombudsman and the National Human Rights Commission. But they are still worshipping in a borrowed hall. Still provisional. Still waiting.

This is not one story. In Teluknaga, Tangerang Regency, the Thessalonika Christian Ecumenical Fellowship Church was sealed by authorities after Good Friday services this year — the second consecutive Easter the congregation has faced eviction. In Riau, the HKBP Kulim Jaya Church faced protests for trying to renovate a building with rotting planks and a leaking roof. The pattern is not bureaucratic delay. The pattern is a message: not here.

Peter and John answered: Acts 4:19-20. Whether it is right in the sight of God to listen to you rather than to God, you must judge, for we cannot but speak of what we have seen and heard. They had been arrested for preaching. They had been threatened. They had been told not to speak. They answered with a grammatical fact: we cannot but speak. The Greek is ou dynatai — it is not possible. Not "we choose not to obey." It is impossible for us to stop. The church in Samarinda Seberang is not defying the government. It is being the church. And being the church is not contingent on paperwork.

The apostles said it plainly: Acts 5:29. We must obey God rather than men. This is not a call to revolution. It is a statement of jurisdiction. The state has authority over permits. God has authority over worship. When the two conflict, the church does not rebel. It simply continues. The Reformers, who worshipped in barns and forests under Mary Tudor, knew this. The Westminster Confession of Faith, 25.3, says Christ gave the ministry, oracles, and ordinances "for the gathering and perfecting of the saints." It does not say He gave them contingent on municipal approval.

Jesus promised: Matthew 16:18. The gates of hell shall not prevail against it. Not the gates of the local planning office. Not the gates of the Interfaith Harmony Forum. The gates of hell. The church's security is not in its permits. It is in its Builder. The Reformers, who built the Protestant church under threat of burning, did not ask for permission. They asked for faithful men. And they built.

Not neglecting to meet together, as is the habit of some, but encouraging one another, and all the more as you see the Day drawing near.

Hebrews 10:25

The church in East Kalimantan meets in a borrowed hall. The church in Teluknaga meets under threat of sealing. The church in Riau meets in a building that leaks. And they meet. They do not neglect to meet together. The author of Hebrews says: let us hold fast the confession of our hope without wavering, for he who promised is faithful. The promise was not a building. The promise was Christ.

The question is not whether the Toraja Church will get its permit. The question is whether the church in the West remembers that its own freedom to build was bought by people who did not have permits. The Reformers did not ask the pope for permission. The Puritans did not ask the crown for permission. The church in China does not ask for permission. The church in Iran does not ask for permission. The church in Indonesia does not ask for permission — it asks for justice, and when justice delays, it preaches anyway.

The church does not need a permit to be the church. The Word and Spirit do. The Builder is faithful. The gates will not prevail. And the congregation in Samarinda Seberang will meet next Sunday, permit or no permit.
