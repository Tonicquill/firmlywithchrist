May 1, 2026

Work is not a curse.

The curse is toil. The gift is vocation. And May Day is older than the revolutionaries.

Vocation Genesis 2:15 Ephesians 4:28

May 1 is Labor Day in most of the world. The marches and banners claim work for the revolutionaries — the communists in Havana, the trade unions in Berlin, the populists everywhere. But the Bible claimed it first. And claimed it differently.

Genesis 2:15: "The LORD God took the man and put him in the garden of Eden to work it and keep it." This is the second chapter of the Bible, before the Fall, before sin entered the world, before capitalism and communism were invented, before the 40-hour week and the gig economy. Work is not punishment. Work is privilege.

The Reformers called this the doctrine of vocation — from the Latin vocare, "to call." Martin Luther argued in The Freedom of a Christian (1520) that a Christian serves God in ordinary work not by adding religious decoration to it but by doing the work itself with integrity. John Calvin, in his Institutes of the Christian Religion, taught that every lawful calling is a station assigned by divine Providence — not merely a way to pay bills but a structure through which God governs human life. Not just pastors have vocations. Farmers. Bakers. Mothers. Coders. Every honest work is sacred because God assigned it, not because it pays well or wins prestige.

Let the thief no longer steal, but rather let him labor, doing honest work with his own hands, so that he may have something to share with anyone in need.

Ephesians 4:28

For the reader who does not know the Bible: the text says work has a goal beyond self-sufficiency. The thief is told to stop taking and start making, with the explicit purpose of having "something to share with anyone in need." The Westminster Larger Catechism calls this a duty of the eighth commandment: "an honest calling, and diligence in it." Work is not about climbing a ladder. It is about producing enough to help the person below you.

The curse in Genesis 3:17–19 is not work itself. The curse is toil — work frustrated, work as exhaustion, work without rest. "By the sweat of your face you shall eat bread." The garden required work: Adam was placed there "to work it and keep it" before sin ever existed. The ground after the Fall required pain. Work is gift. Toil is curse. The Christian who works an honest job is not cursed. They are doing what humans were made to do.

Exodus 20:9–10: "Six days you shall labor, and do all your work, but the seventh day is a Sabbath to the LORD your God." God commands rest because He knows humans will work themselves to death without it. The Sabbath is not anti-work. It is the completion of work. Rest is not earned by six days of labor. It is commanded as a gift. Genesis 2:2 says God Himself rested on the seventh day. Humans rest not because they have worked enough but because their Creator rested first.

So when the banners march today, the Christian does not need to choose between capitalism and socialism. The Christian needs to remember: work is a gift. Rest is commanded. And both exist for the glory of God.
