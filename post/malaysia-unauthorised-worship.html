Your church meets in a shop lot. Acts 2:46 says that's where it started.

Malaysia calls them 'unauthorised.' The Bible calls them 'the church.' The house church is not an emergency measure. It is the original.

Ecclesiology Acts 2:46 Matthew 18:20 Romans 16:5 1 Corinthians 16:19 Philemon 1:2

Malaysian Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim called on local councils in early 2026 to address "unauthorised" places of worship. The Malaysian Consultative Council of Buddhism, Christianity, Hinduism, Sikhism, and Taoism — MCCBCHST — is seeking clarification. Many non-Muslim places of worship predate modern land codes. Others operate in shop lots, warehouses, and pastors' homes out of necessity. Christian leaders warn that rigid enforcement could displace urban congregations and Orang Asli village churches without distinguishing between deliberately illegal structures and long-standing community churches that lack formal registration.

The fear is not abstract. It is the fear that your building will be declared illegal not because it is wrong, but because it is inconvenient. That bureaucratic compliance is being used as a tool to suppress minority faith. That the church will be told to move, again, and there will be nowhere to go.

Luke wrote: Acts 2:46. Day by day, attending the temple together and breaking bread in their homes. The early church did not have cathedrals. It had dining rooms. The house church is not a downgrade. It is the original. The apostle Paul greets: Romans 16:5, 1 Corinthians 16:19, Philemon 1:2. The church in your house. Not the church that owns a house. The church that is a house. A living room, a table, a shared meal, the Word read aloud. This is not an emergency measure. It is the biblical norm.

The Reformers knew this. When Mary Tudor burned Protestants and banned their assemblies, the English church did not disappear. It moved into fields, caves, ship holds, and private homes. When the French Huguenots were stripped of their churches, they met in barns and forests. The church does not need a steeple. It needs the Word, the sacraments, and faithful people. The Westminster Confession of Faith, 25.3, says Christ gave the ministry, oracles, and ordinances "for the gathering and perfecting of the saints." It does not say He gave them for the impressing of the magistrate.

For where two or three are gathered in my name, there am I among them.

Matthew 18:20

Jesus said: Matthew 18:20. Where two or three are gathered. Not where two or three are zoned. Not where two or three are registered. Gathered in my name. The church's legitimacy is Christological, not municipal. The shop lot in Kuala Lumpur is as much a church as Notre-Dame, because Christ is as present in the shop lot as He is in the cathedral. The Reformers, who fought the Roman Catholic Church's claim to exclusive sacred space, would recognize the Malaysian government's claim as the same error in a different accent: the belief that holiness requires official approval.

The Malaysian government calls these spaces "unauthorised." The Bible calls them "the church." The Orang Asli village church meeting under a roof of palm fronds is the church. The urban congregation renting a shop lot above a restaurant is the church. The pastor's living room with twenty folding chairs is the church. Not because they have permits. Because they have Christ. And Christ does not issue permits. He issues promises.

The shop lot in Kuala Lumpur is as much a church as Notre-Dame, because Christ is as present in the shop lot as He is in the cathedral.
