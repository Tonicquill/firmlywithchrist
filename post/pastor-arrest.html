"In the name of Jesus, get in the car."

UK pastor arrested for street preaching in Watford. When the state says stop, Scripture says obey God rather than men. A Reformed theology take on persecution.

Persecution Acts 5:29 Isaiah 33:22

Steve Maile is 66 years old. He is a grandfather. He pastors Oasis City Church in Watford, England. On April 18, while preaching the gospel in the town centre, he was handcuffed so tightly he needed medical splints afterward, bundled into a police car while his family watched, and mocked by an officer who told him, "In the name of Jesus, get in the car."

The charge was a Section 5 public order offence. The real offence was saying that Jesus is Lord in a public square that belongs to Him.

This is not a human rights story. It is a sovereignty story.

The state operates as if authority rises from the ballot box or the police station. The Bible says something older and more terrifying.

For the LORD is our Judge, the LORD is our Lawgiver, the LORD is our King; He will save us.

Isaiah 33:22

God does not share His throne. Human governments are His ministers, not His rivals. They possess delegated authority, strictly limited by His decree. When a magistrate forbids what God commands, the magistrate has not issued a law. He has issued a blasphemy.

Peter and the apostles faced the same thing. The Sanhedrin, the highest religious court in Israel, ordered them to stop preaching. Their response was not a petition for religious liberty. It was a declaration of war on the wrong side.

But Peter and the other apostles answered and said: "We ought to obey God rather than men."

Acts 5:29

To stop preaching because a human official says so is nothing short of mutiny against Christ's headship.

But here is where Reformed theology refuses the easy narrative. We do not say that God was surprised by this arrest, or that Satan outmaneuvered Him. We say what the early church prayed after Herod and Pontius Pilate conspired to murder Jesus:

For truly against Your holy Servant Jesus, whom You anointed, both Herod and Pontius Pilate, with the Gentiles and the people of Israel, were gathered together to do whatever Your hand and Your purpose determined before to be done.

Acts 4:27-28

The same hand that holds the galaxies holds the Hertfordshire police badge. This is not fatalism. It is providence. God personally, exhaustively, and meticulously governs every aspect of His creation. The state opposition to the gospel is permitted by God for His own wise and holy ends. Just as the worst atrocity of human history, the crucifixion, was determined beforehand by God to become the greatest good, so also the harassment of a street preacher in Watford is woven into a pattern we will only fully read in glory.

So we do not panic. We do not draft petitions as if the outcome were uncertain. We do not treat the British government as a force that can ultimately silence the gospel. 2 Timothy 2:9 says the word of God is not chained.

John Huss, burned at the stake for refusing to bow to papal utterances that contradicted Scripture, taught that "to resist him is not a sin, it is a mandate." Martin Luther, standing before his accusers at the Diet of Worms, said: "Unless I am convicted by Scripture and plain reason, my conscience is captive to the Word of God. Here I stand." John Calvin wrote that Christ alone is the Head over all things to the church, and we must bid farewell to any authority that would usurp His preeminence.

They were not fighting for religious freedom. They were fighting for the freedom of God to be God.

Peter tells the church: "But even if you should suffer for righteousness' sake, you are blessed. 'And do not be afraid of their threats, nor be troubled'" 1 Peter 3:14. Not "if you suffer, demand better treatment." Not "if you suffer, start a social media campaign." Suffer for righteousness' sake, and be blessed. Rejoice to the extent that you partake of Christ's sufferings. Your labor is not in vain in the Lord.

The officer who mocked Steve Maile with the name of Jesus made a theological statement he did not understand. He thought the name of Jesus was a tool of compliance, a spell to make a troublesome preacher get in the car. He treated the name of the risen King like a parking ticket.

He will learn better. Every knee will bow, and every tongue will confess that Jesus Christ is Lord. The question is not whether Jesus is King. The question is whether the Hertfordshire Police, the Crown Prosecution Service, and every other human court will recognize it now, or on the day when recognition comes too late.

Steve Maile says he will not stop preaching, even if he faces prison. Good. The state may have the keys to a cell, but it does not have the keys to death and Hades. Those belong to Christ. And Christ has already determined that His gospel will be preached in season and out of season, by men who obey God rather than men, until the earth is filled with the knowledge of the glory of the LORD as the waters cover the sea.

The state may have the keys to a cell. Christ has the keys to death and Hades. Preach anyway.
