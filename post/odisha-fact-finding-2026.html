The fact-finding team went to Odisha. They found the same hell as 2008.

Beatings. Sexual assault. Burned homes. Police refusing to file FIRs. A Christian fact-finding team documented Kandhamal district 18 years after the pogrom. The pattern is unchanged.

Persecution Acts 14:22 2 Timothy 3:12 Acts 5:29 Revelation 12:11 1 Samuel 2:10 Matthew 28:18

A Christian fact-finding team visited Kandhamal district, Odisha, this month. What they documented belongs to a genre the church has been reading for two thousand years. Believers beaten with iron rods. Women sexually assaulted. Pastors' homes burned to the ground. Police officers who refuse to file First Information Reports. False cases registered against victims while perpetrators walk free. This is not a new crisis. It is the 18th year of the same crisis. The 2008 anti-Christian pogrom killed approximately 100 people and displaced 50,000. The pattern has not changed. Only the date on the report has changed.

The international press calls it "communal tension." The Indian government calls it "isolated incidents." The church calls it what Scripture calls it: persecution. And persecution, in the biblical framework, is not a policy failure to be corrected. It is a mark of the church to be expected.

This is what the apostles signed up for

Paul and Barnabas circled back through the cities where Paul had been stoned and left for dead — Lystra, Iconium, Antioch — and told the believers something the modern Western church has erased from its operating manual: "Through many tribulations we must enter the kingdom of God" (Acts 14:22). The preposition is not "despite." It is "through." Tribulation is not an obstacle on the way to the kingdom. It is the road itself.

Paul sharpens the point in his final letter: "All who desire to live a godly life in Christ Jesus will be persecuted" (2 Timothy 3:12). Not "some." Not "those in exceptionally hostile contexts." All. The promise is universal. If the church in the West is not experiencing what the church in Odisha is experiencing, the question is not "why is God allowing this to happen to them?" The question is "why is it not happening to us?"

The police officer answers to a higher jurisdiction

When the Sanhedrin commanded the apostles to stop teaching in Jesus' name, Peter's reply established the irreducible priority of the church for all time: "We must obey God rather than men" (Acts 5:29). The state has legitimate authority within its sphere. But the state's sphere does not extend to the suppression of the gospel, the protection of criminals who attack Christ's body, or the refusal to register crimes committed against Christians because the victims are the wrong religion.

The police officer in Kandhamal who refuses to file an FIR when a pastor's home is burned is not merely failing at his job. He is using delegated state power to persecute the church. And God's jurisdiction over that officer is not diminished by the officer's refusal to acknowledge it. The same Christ who said "render to Caesar" also said "all authority in heaven and on earth has been given to me" (Matthew 28:18). Caesar's authority is real, derivative, delegated, and accountable. The officer will give an account.

The weapons that actually work

The apocalyptic vision in Revelation names the church's arsenal: "They have conquered him by the blood of the Lamb and by the word of their testimony, for they loved not their lives even unto death" (Revelation 12:11). Not by the blood of the militia. Not by the word of the diplomat. Not by the FIR that was finally filed after international outcry. The blood of the Lamb. The word of their testimony. These are not metaphors for powerlessness. They are the only weapons that have ever defeated the principalities.

The church in Odisha has been conquering this way for 18 years — without an army, without a lobbyist in Delhi, without a single conviction in the courts. The weapons have not changed. The victory has not been revoked. The Hindu nationalist who burns a pastor's home today will face the same Judge as every other persecutor in history, and "the adversaries of the LORD shall be broken to pieces; against them He will thunder in heaven" (1 Samuel 2:10). There is no statute of limitations in the divine courtroom.

The church in Odisha has been conquering for 18 years — without an army, without a lobbyist, without a conviction. The weapons have not changed. The blood of the Lamb. The word of their testimony. And the gates of hell are not prevailing.
