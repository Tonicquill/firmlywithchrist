160 Christians in East Kalimantan did everything the government asked. The permit never came. The Reformers would recognize this.

The Toraja Church in Samarinda Seberang, East Kalimantan, has 160 members. They applied for a building permit in 2023. They obtained a recommendation from the Interfaith Harmony Forum in September 2024. They obtained approval from the Ministry of Religious Affairs in April 2025. Every document the Indonesian government asked for, they submitted. The permit never came.

The local government says some community groups oppose the church. The congregation says that is discrimination. In Teluknaga, Tangerang Regency, the Thessalonika Christian Ecumenical Fellowship Church was sealed by authorities after Good Friday services — the second consecutive Easter the congregation has faced eviction. In Riau, the HKBP Kulim Jaya Church faced protests for trying to renovate a building with rotting planks and a leaking roof. The pattern is not bureaucratic delay. The pattern is a message: not here.

We must obey God rather than men

When the Sanhedrin commanded the apostles to stop preaching, Peter answered with the irreducible principle of Christian political theology: Acts 5:29. Not "we must negotiate with men." Not "we must seek a reasonable accommodation with men." Obey. God. Rather. Than. Men. The Greek is peitharchein — to obey one in authority, to follow one's sovereign command. The apostles were not anarchists. They were making a jurisdictional claim. When the state demands what God forbids, or forbids what God commands, the believer has no moral option. The obligation is singular.

The Toraja Church is not defying the government. It is obeying Christ. The command to gather, to preach, to administer the sacraments cannot be suspended by a municipal planning office. The Reformers who worshipped in barns and forests under Mary Tudor were not rebels. They were the most obedient Christians in England — obedient to a throne higher than the crown.

The LORD is Judge, Lawgiver, and King

Isaiah 33:22 declares the Triune God's exclusive threefold office. No president, no governor, no district official can occupy any of these roles over the conscience of the believer. The Westminster Confession of Faith, 20.2, teaches that "God alone is Lord of the conscience, and hath left it free from the doctrines and commandments of men which are in anything contrary to His Word." When the Interfaith Harmony Forum issues a recommendation that the church then complies with, and the permit still never comes, the machinery is exposed: the process was never about harmony. It was about control.

Hannah's prayer cuts through the pretense: "The LORD makes poor and makes rich; He brings low and lifts up" (1 Samuel 2:7). The hand that closes the permit office is the same hand that opened the Red Sea. Sovereignty does not mean the permit will arrive. It means the delay is governed, the officials are governed, the outcome is governed. The Triune King is not waiting for the outcome of the Ombudsman's investigation.

An indestructible spiritual temple

Christ's promise in Matthew 16:18 — that the gates of hell will not prevail against His church — is not a promise about building codes. It is a promise about ontology. The church is a spiritual temple made of living stones (1 Peter 2:5). Municipal approval cannot create it. Municipal denial cannot destroy it. The congregation in Samarinda Seberang meeting in a borrowed hall is as much the church as any cathedral in Europe, because Christ is as present in the borrowed hall as He is beneath any consecrated spire.

The Reformers understood this. When Calvin's Geneva stripped the churches of images but filled them with psalms, the move was not aesthetic. It was jurisdictional. The church does not belong to the planning office, the Interfaith Harmony Forum, or the Ministry of Religious Affairs. It belongs to Christ.

The jurisdiction of the King's Word

Christ's Great Commission does not include an application process. "Go therefore and make disciples of all nations" (Matthew 28:19–20) — this mandate precedes every human government and outlasts them all. The church in East Kalimantan is operating under a jurisdiction more ancient and more permanent than the Republic of Indonesia.

"Not neglecting to meet together, as is the habit of some, but encouraging one another, and all the more as you see the Day drawing near" (Hebrews 10:25). The church meets. Permit or no permit. Because the command to gather issues from a throne no municipal council has ever approached.

The church does not need a permit to be the church. The command to gather issues from a throne no municipal council has ever approached.
