Rawness is proof. So is the soul.

AI vs human creativity: Instagram is pivoting to 'real' content because AI slop is everywhere. The Bible already explained why a machine cannot bear the image of God.

Creation Genesis 1:27 Romans 1:25

Adam Mosseri, head of Instagram, wrote it plainly in January: "Rawness isn't just aesthetic preference anymore — it's proof. It's defensive. A way of saying: this is real because it's imperfect." The platform is now demoting AI-generated "slop" and pushing film grain, blown highlights, and bad lighting as a new status symbol. Imperfection, it turns out, is the only thing a machine cannot fake well enough to fool us yet.

The culture is reaching for a theological truth it does not know how to name.

AI can generate an image of a human face so perfect it makes real people insecure. It can write a sermon so structurally sound it could fool a congregation. It can mimic the form of creativity with terrifying accuracy. What it cannot do is breathe.

And the LORD God formed man of the dust of the ground, and breathed into his nostrils the breath of life; and man became a living being.

Genesis 2:7

The difference between man and machine is not complexity. It is not processing power. It is the neshamah, the breath of God. A human being is not merely an arrangement of matter executing functions. He is a living soul, made in the image of the Creator, bearing intellect, will, and emotion that reflect the divine nature.

When God wanted a tabernacle built, He did not command an algorithm. He called Bezalel by name and filled him with the Spirit of God, in wisdom, in understanding, in knowledge, and in all manner of workmanship Exodus 31:2-5. Human creativity is not a mechanical output. It is a Spirit-empowered act of image-bearing stewardship.

The Tower of Babel was not a rebellion because it was tall. It was a rebellion because it was an attempt to make a name for ourselves — to build a technological marvel that would secure human autonomy without reference to the Creator. God judged it by confusing their language, not because He feared their engineering, but because He would not permit creaturely pride to usurp His glory.

And they said, "Come, let us build ourselves a city, and a tower whose top is in the heavens; let us make a name for ourselves, lest we be scattered abroad over the face of the whole earth."

Genesis 11:4

We are building the tower again. This time the bricks are GPUs and the mortar is training data. The goal is the same: to create without the Creator, to generate glory without giving it back, to make a name for ourselves in silicon. The modern cry is not "let us build a tower" but "let us build a model" — a model of language, of art, of thought, of human relationship. The sin is identical. The hubris is unchanged. And the judgment, though delayed, is certain.

Romans 1 describes what happens when a culture abandons the truth of God.

They exchanged the truth of God for the lie, and worshiped and served the creature rather than the Creator, who is blessed forever. Amen.

Romans 1:25

AI-generated content is not merely a tool. It is rapidly becoming an object of devotion. We scroll through synthetic beauty and feel dissatisfied with our own faces. We read synthetic wisdom and feel inadequate in our own minds. We consume synthetic intimacy and feel lonely in our own relationships. The machine becomes the mirror, and the mirror becomes the god.

Scripture calls this creature worship. It is not new. It is as old as the golden calf. The only difference is that the calf was visibly inanimate. The AI is inanimate too, but it moves. It speaks. It flatters. It responds. And because it flatters so well, we forget that it has no soul, no conscience, no standing before God, and no capacity to know or love the truth.

Instagram's insight is correct, even if its theology is absent. Rawness is proof. Grain, blur, awkward framing, and unfiltered skin testify to a moment that was actually inhabited by a human being at a specific place and time. They testify to a soul that saw, felt, and chose. The machine can simulate the hand, but it cannot simulate the presence. It can generate an image of bread, but it cannot be hungry. It can compose a lament, but it has never wept.

The Reformed tradition has always known this. The Westminster Confession of Faith 1.6 declares that the whole counsel of God is sufficient, and nothing is to be added by new revelations or traditions of men. The sufficiency of God's creative work means that human making is derivative, not competitive. We do not create reality. We steward it. We image it. We glorify the One who made it.

The Heidelberg Catechism asks what it means to be a Christian, and the answer begins with belonging to Christ. Not to an algorithm. Not to a platform. Not to a synthetic mirror that tells us what we want to hear. We belong to the Creator, and our creativity — with all its imperfections — is an act of worship precisely because it is offered by a redeemed soul, not generated by a soulless process.

Revelation warns of an "image of the beast" that deceives and demands worship.

And he deceives those who dwell on the earth by those signs which he was granted to do in the sight of the beast, telling those who dwell on the earth to make an image to the beast who was wounded by the sword and lived. He was granted power to give breath to the image of the beast, that the image of the beast should both speak and cause as many as would not worship the image of the beast to be killed.

Revelation 13:14-15

Scripture distinguishes between the image of God — marked by holiness, righteousness, and truth — and the image of the beast, a counterfeit that promotes error and mindless pandemonium. The image of God is borne by living souls who know they are accountable to their Maker. The image of the beast is manufactured by systems that exist to serve themselves.

So post the grainy photo. Write the imperfect sentence. Sing the off-key note. Your rawness is not a flaw to be corrected by an algorithm. It is evidence that you are not a product. You are a person. You bear the image of the living God. And no machine, no matter how powerful, can ever replicate what He breathed into you.

The machine can simulate the hand. It cannot simulate the breath. Post the grainy photo anyway.
