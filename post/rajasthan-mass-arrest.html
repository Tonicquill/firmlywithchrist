They stormed the Mass with knives. The police arrested the Catholics.

A mob armed with knives interrupted a Sunday Mass in Rajasthan. The police filed charges — against the Catholics. Acts 4:19-20 was written for this moment.

Persecution Acts 4:19-20 Acts 5:29 1 Samuel 2:6-10 Matthew 16:18

On a Sunday morning in Rajasthan, a group of men armed with knives stormed a Catholic Mass. The congregation fled. The intruders vandalized the sanctuary. When the police arrived, they arrested the Catholics. Not the men with knives. Not the men who interrupted a religious service with weapons in hand. The Catholics — booked for "unlawful assembly" and "disturbing the peace." Let that settle for a moment. A congregation gathered to worship. Armed men broke in to attack them. The state's response was to process the victims as criminals and let the attackers watch from the street.

This is not incompetence. It is not a misunderstanding. It is a legal technology designed to produce exactly this outcome, and it runs on Section 295A of the Indian Penal Code — "deliberate and malicious acts intended to outrage religious feelings." The mechanism is elegant in its cruelty: the Hindu nationalist mob's feelings are, by legal definition, outraged by the existence of Christian worship. The Christians, by worshipping, have "outraged" those feelings. The mob attacks. The victims file a complaint. The police, who almost certainly share the mob's religion, counter-file under 295A. The victims become the accused. The original crime — armed men with knives storming a church — vanishes from the record. The only thing left is a case file where the Catholics are the defendants.

I described this exact mechanism in yesterday's piece on Odisha. It is the same mechanism in Rajasthan. It will be the same mechanism next week in another state, and the week after in another. The names change. The technology does not.

The law that makes the victim the criminal

Peter and John faced the same structure in Jerusalem. The Sanhedrin — the highest religious-legal authority in Israel — ordered them to stop speaking in Jesus' name. The order was legally valid under the system that issued it. Peter's response did not dispute the legality. It disputed the jurisdiction: "Whether it is right in the sight of God to listen to you rather than to God, you must judge, for we cannot but speak of what we have seen and heard" (Acts 4:19–20). There is a higher court. The Sanhedrin's ruling was legally valid and theologically void. The Rajasthan police's FIR is legally valid and theologically void. The structure is identical. Only the uniforms have changed.

The same apostles, arrested again, sharpened the point to a single sentence: "We must obey God rather than men" (Acts 5:29). This is not a slogan for the comfortable. It is operational doctrine for the church in India. The FIR is real. The charges under 295A are real. The jail time is real. None of it changes what the church will do next Sunday morning, and the Sunday after that, and the Sunday after that.

The Judge who cannot be fooled by paperwork

The police officer who filed the false charges, the prosecutor who signed the document, the mob leader who wielded the knife, the politician who campaigned on "protecting the faith" while enabling systematic attacks on churches — every one of them breathes borrowed air. Every one of them will give an account to a Judge whose rulings cannot be appealed and whose docket cannot be manipulated by a counter-filed FIR. "The adversaries of the LORD shall be broken to pieces; against them He will thunder in heaven" (1 Samuel 2:10). The thunder is not a metaphor. The breaking is not symbolic. The judgment is not subject to Section 295A.

The church in India worships in buildings when it can, in homes when it must, in jail when the state demands it. The location does not change the worship. The charges do not change the confession. The knives do not change the outcome. "On this rock I will build My church, and the gates of hell shall not prevail against it" (Matthew 16:18). The gates of hell include the police stations, the courtrooms, and the mobs with knives. None of them will prevail. None of them have.

They stormed the Mass with knives. The police arrested the Catholics. The mechanism is designed. The Judge of all the earth will not be fooled by a counter-filed FIR.
