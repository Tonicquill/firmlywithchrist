The Holy Mother of God Cathedral in Stepanakert, Nagorno-Karabakh, was consecrated in 1889. It survived the Soviet era, the war of the 1990s, and the 2020 conflict. It did not survive April 2026. Azerbaijani forces demolished it. The St. Jacob Church was destroyed alongside it. The European Parliament adopted a resolution April 30 demanding accountability. Azerbaijan's parliament voted May 1 to sever ties with the European Parliament entirely. Diplomats talked. The cathedral fell.

The European Centre for Law and Justice records over 2,200 anti-Christian incidents in Europe in 2024 — vandalism, arson, murder of clergy. The Council of Europe passed a resolution on religious discrimination naming antisemitism and Islamophobia. It did not name anti-Christian prejudice. The cathedral is being erased twice: first by bulldozers, then by institutional silence.

They have set Your sanctuary on fire

The psalmist's cry in Psalm 74:7–8 is not metaphor. This is the vocabulary the Bible gives for exactly this event. "They have profaned the dwelling place of Your name to the ground. They said in their hearts, 'Let us destroy them altogether.'" Physical sanctuaries are meeting places where the Word of God is preached, explained, applied, and obeyed. While God does not dwell in temples made with hands, the erasure of a cathedral is a serious crime because it seeks to silence the voice of the Chief Shepherd to His sheep.

The psalmist does not say "buildings don't matter." He says their destruction is an attack on God's Name. The destruction in Nagorno-Karabakh mirrors the unrighteous deception of those who "suppress the truth in unrighteousness." You can find tranquility of soul by recognizing that no bulldozer can derail God's eternal purposes for His people.

The nations have entered her sanctuary

Lamentations 1:10 records the profanation: "The adversary has spread his hand over all her pleasant things." The institutional silence of bodies like the Council of Europe — which names every prejudice except that against Christ's people — reflects the practical atheism of a fallen world. Reformed theology warns that human logic is ruled by pride and material gain makes liars out of men, leading to the unrighteous witness of those who ignore the message of the oppressed.

Never become so involved in this world that you seek deliverance from a resolution or an international board rather than the King. Your sole objective is to be faithful with what you have been given, recognizing that while the nations rage, the LORD has established His throne in heaven. The church does not need the European Parliament's attention. She has a higher court.

Devout men carried Stephen and made great lamentation over him

Acts 8:2 records the church's response to its first martyr: "Devout men carried Stephen to his burial, and made great lamentation over him." The Scripture gives you permission to weep. The grief and anxiety that characterize the emotional world of man are not a lack of faith — the heroes of church history met destruction not with stoic indifference but with godly sorrow. When you feel burdened by the sight of rubble, do not lean on your own understanding or trust your own fluctuating, unreliable feelings. Cast your burden on the LORD, knowing that He heals the brokenhearted and binds up their wounds.

Your scars don't define you. Your hope does. The lament is a righteous rebellion against the notion that the erasure of God's house is normal or inevitable.

On this rock I will build My Church

Christ's promise in Matthew 16:18 — that the gates of Hades will not prevail — rests on sovereign elective purposes rather than municipal status or parliamentary resolutions. Reformed ecclesiology affirms that the church is a supernatural work of God, a spiritual temple made of living stones. An empire may erase stone, but they cannot touch the quality of life which forms the soul or the unsearchable riches of Christ. The church's destiny is secure because its Builder is faithful.

Do not be worried by the fearsome enemy or the daunting dangers. He who has begun a good work in you will complete it. Trade in your worry for trust in the Rock of Ages, knowing that nothing thwarts any aspect of His plan.

The cathedral fell. The church did not. And the church will outlive every empire that thought erasing stone would erase the Name.
