On May 4, 2026, Melkite Greek Catholic bishops in Lebanon condemned the destruction of a Melkite convent in the village of Yaroun, southern Lebanon — reportedly demolished by Israeli forces operating in the area. Two days later, Hezbollah supporters launched a campaign of abuse against Syriac Maronite Patriarch Mar Bechara Boutros al-Rai, accusing him of treason and collaboration with Israel. Eastern patriarchs — Assyrian Church of the East, Syriac Catholic, Chaldean — condemned the attacks as an attempt to incite sectarian strife.

The church in the Middle East is being squeezed from both sides. Physical destruction on one flank. Rhetorical destruction on the other. The expectation is that Christians will pick a team in a war they did not start. The reality is that the church is not a team, not a nation, and does not have a foreign policy.

My kingdom is not of this world

Standing before Pilate — the representative of the world's most powerful empire — Jesus declared: John 18:36. "If My kingdom were of this world, My servants would fight." The church in Lebanon has no tanks, no lobby in Washington or Tehran, no militia. It has the Word, the sacraments, and the promise that the gates of hell will not prevail. When Patriarch al-Rai is attacked for not being political enough, the irony cuts: his actual job is to be theological, not political. The Westminster Confession of Faith, 25.3, says the church's mandate is the ministry, oracles, and ordinances — not geopolitical alignment, not partisan endorsement, not picking a side in a war Christ has already transcended.

The convent in Yaroun is not Lebanese property or Israeli property. It is church property. And the church belongs to Christ.

They have conquered by the blood of the Lamb

The apocalyptic vision in Revelation 12:11 reveals the weapons of the church: "They have conquered him by the blood of the Lamb and by the word of their testimony." Not by the blood of the militia. Not by the word of the diplomat. The blood of the Lamb. The word of their testimony. The church in the Middle East has been conquering this way for two millennia — through empires that rose and fell, through caliphates that rose and fell, through mandates and wars and revolutions. The weapons have not changed. The victory has not been revoked.

When Hezbollah's supporters demand that the patriarch align, they are demanding that he trade his actual weapon — his testimony — for a counterfeit one. The church does not fight with the tools of the principalities. It fights with a Lamb and a word, and it wins.

The LORD kills and makes alive

Hannah's war cry in 1 Samuel 2:6–7 declares Yahweh sovereign over every demolition, every accusation of treason, every "institutional silence" that follows. "The LORD kills and makes alive; He brings down to the grave and brings up." The verb — kills — is not softened. The demolition crew in Yaroun, the Hezbollah supporter typing abuse on his phone, the diplomat who wrote a resolution that named everything except the attack on Christ's church — all of them breathe borrowed air, maintained by the God whose Name they are silencing. The noise and tumult of Lebanon's political chaos is not outside God's administrative order. It is entirely within it.

You can find tranquility of soul by recognizing that the hand of God is behind every blessing and every disaster, and that no bulldozer can derail His eternal purposes for His people.

We must obey God rather than men

The apostolic rule was established in Jerusalem before the Sanhedrin: Acts 5:29. When the powers of the earth demand that the church pick a side — Israeli or Hezbollah, West or East, Resolution A or Resolution B — the church answers with a single irreducible priority: obey God. This is not a refusal to engage. It is a jurisdictional declaration. The church is not above the authorities. But it is not under them either. It is under Christ.

"Here we have no lasting city, but we seek the city that is to come" (Hebrews 13:14). The church in Lebanon is not building a lasting city in Yaroun. It is building a testimony. The convent was a witness. Its destruction is a witness. The patriarch's refusal to pick a side is a witness. The Eastern patriarchs' solidarity is a witness. And the testimony will outlive every army that thought burning stone would silence the Word.

The church in the Middle East has no army. It has the blood of the Lamb and the word of its testimony. And it has outlived every empire that demanded it pick a side.
