Editorial Standards

How we write, why we write, and what keeps the work honest.

About 2 Timothy 2:15

Firmly With Christ is a confessionally Reformed publication that applies theology to current events, culture, and the questions people are actually asking. We are not a devotional site, a church bulletin, or a news aggregator. We are a publication that believes doctrine is meant to be thought with — not just recited.

Because we write about theology in a public space, and because theological content shapes how people think about suffering, justice, authority, and hope, we take accuracy and transparency seriously. This page documents our standards so readers can judge the work for themselves.

1. Confessional Alignment

We write from the Reformed tradition, specifically:

  • The Westminster Confession of Faith (1647)
  • The Three Forms of Unity (Heidelberg Catechism, Belgic Confession, Canons of Dort)
  • The 1689 London Baptist Confession of Faith

We do not claim to represent all Reformed traditions, nor do we speak for any denomination. We claim alignment with the confessions above, and we cite them explicitly when grounding a claim. If a piece touches a topic where Reformed Christians disagree (eschatology, theonomy, paedocommunion), we state our position and move on — without pretending the disagreement does not exist.

2. Source Policy

Bible translations: We use the English Standard Version (ESV) as our default. When another translation clarifies a point, we note it.

Confessional documents: We cite chapter and section. Every confessional reference includes a hyperlink to the primary text (westminsterconfession.org, creeds.net, or equivalent).

Historical sources: We prefer primary texts over secondary summaries. When citing Calvin, Edwards, Owen, or others, we reference the specific work, book, and chapter where possible. When we rely on secondary scholarship, we name the scholar and the work.

Contemporary sources: News-anchored pieces rely on established reporting (Reuters, BBC, Associated Press, specialist outlets like Anglican Ink or International Christian Concern). We do not rely on viral social media clips as primary evidence. Every source is hyperlinked in the Sources section of the piece.

NLM corpus: We maintain a theological reference corpus in NotebookLM (notebooklm.google.com) containing primary Reformed texts, systematic theology, and biblical commentaries. This corpus is consulted before drafting and after drafting to verify claims.

3. Quality Control Process

Every piece passes three gates before publication:

  1. NLM theological verification. The draft is queried against our theological corpus to confirm confessional alignment, catch accidental heterodoxy, and ensure biblical claims are accurately represented.
  2. The curious-secularist read. Would a reader who does not share the Reformed frame finish the piece without feeling preached at? The test is not agreement — it is recognition. Does the reader see a thinker addressing their world, or a cleric addressing the unsaved?
  3. The deconstruction-survivor read. Would someone who left an abusive evangelical environment find this disarming or triggering? Confidence and conviction are fine. Pulpit cadence, prescriptive verbs, and gatekeeping language are not.

If a piece fails any gate, it goes back to draft. No exceptions.

4. Correction Policy

We correct substantive errors openly and promptly. A substantive error is: a misquoted Bible verse, an incorrect confessional citation, a factual error in the news reporting, or a theological claim that misrepresents the tradition. Typos and stylistic edits are not logged.

Corrections are appended to the piece with a dated note and a brief explanation. They are also logged below.

6. Authorial Voice

This site is authored under a pen name. The writer is a confessionally Reformed layperson who has chosen to write pseudonymously to keep the focus on the substance rather than the personality. Pseudonymity is stated openly because transparency about authorship — even limited transparency — is more trustworthy than apparent anonymity.

We do not accept guest posts, sponsored content, or affiliate-driven theology. Every piece is written, edited, and published by the same editorial voice.

If you believe we have misrepresented a text, misstated a fact, or failed our own standards, contact us through the channels linked in the footer. We read every message. If you are right, we will correct it and log it here.
